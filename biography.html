About Taban Peter Pal

Taban Peter Pal (born March 6th 2005) is a South Sudanese artist working in a genre of art known as hyperrealism.

He is known for creating realistic portraits of people mostly of African decent.

Working primarily with charcoal and graphite on paper. Taban’s drawings depict figures exhibiting emotions.

He uses his works to bring awareness about things happening around him and are relatable all over the world and also expressing cultural variations of different groups of people.

Starting at a very tender age, Taban has always been enthusiastic about drawing realistic portraits on paper and other mediums like cardboard.

Being exposed to various art television programs at a young age, Taban, grew to love art. Over the years, he gradually taught himself how to master the art of hyperrealism through re-drawing and copying artworks of several hyperrealism artists.

With no formal training, he spends over 80 hours or about 2 weeks per art piece
